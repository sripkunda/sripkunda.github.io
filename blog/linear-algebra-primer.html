Linear Algebra Primer

This article is a fast paced introduction to Linear Algebra.

Linear algebra is the study of linear relationships, linear maps, and their many representations. There are countless applications in various subjects in computer science, engineering, physics, and (naturally) mathematics. Linear algebra has been cleverly applied to other seemingly unrelated fields as well. To see the power of this subject, it may be worth looking into Google's PageRank Algorithm.

The treatment leaves out details for the sake of speed and understanding, as it is meant to be a quick introduction to the topic. The prerequisites are single variable calculus, although enough mathematical maturity should get you through most of the content. For a review of integration, see my article on integrals.

Many things I want to write about require linear algebra, so I wrote this primarily as a reference to make my other writings more accessible. The content is based on the books Linear Algebra Done Right by S. Axler and Linear and Geometric Algebra by A. Macdonald, although alternative approaches are taken for some topics.
